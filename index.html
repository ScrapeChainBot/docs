ScrapeChain / Introduction

ScrapeChain Bot

ScrapeChain is a self hosted tool that reads token addresses from your choosen bots, channels, groups and automatically executes trades on Solana and Robinhood with advanced filtering systems from User filtering to contract address filters to message filters. Scrapechain is hosted on cloud for 24/7 reliability. No more missed entries.

What is ScrapeChain?

ScrapeChain is your edge. If you’ve ever missed a profitable trade because you were too slow to copy-paste a contract, ScrapeChain solves that completely. If you ever needed a tool that could filter words/numbers in a non-static manner from a structured channels so you only buy winners, here it is. It continuously monitors your favorite channels, filters out scam tokens with built-in safety shields, and executes buys in milliseconds before the market even reacts.

Supported Chains

ChainTypeExecution
◎ Solana (SOL)L1Via GMGN with Jito bundle support
🟢 RobinhoodEVM L2Via your QuickNode RPC endpoint

How it works

  1. You add Telegram channels/groups to the bot's Source Monitor.
  2. When a message containing a valid contract address appears, the bot validates it against your safety filters.
  3. If it passes, the bot buys automatically using your configured amount, slippage, and fee settings.
  4. The bot then monitors the position and sells when your Stop Loss, Take Profit, or Rug Shield conditions are met.
Bot link Access the bot at https://t.me/ScrapeChainBot

Duplicate Detection

Prevents the bot from buying the same token contract address (CA) more than once within a given window.

If the same contract address appears in multiple messages — across different channels or at different times — Duplicate Detection ensures you only enter once. This protects you from accidentally stacking positions on the same token.

Permanent

The bot records every CA it has ever bought and will never buy it again. There is no expiry — the block is permanent. (The bot does clean up records older than 1 year in the background, but in practice a CA will almost never resurface that long after launch.)

Recommended for most users Permanent mode means each token is a one-time trade — you get in, and any future signals for that same CA are ignored. This is the default.

Timed

Blocks a CA for a specific duration. After the timer expires, the same CA can trigger a fresh buy again.

OptionBlock Duration
1 Day24 hours
1 Week7 days
CustomAny duration using format: 72H, 2W, 30M, 3D

Custom format: Enter a number followed by M (minutes), H (hours), D (days), or W (weeks).

When to use Timed: Useful if you trade channels that call the same token at different times and you want the option to re-enter later. For most users tracking fresh launches, Permanent is the better choice.

Note on switching modes Switching from Timed back to Permanent does not clear existing timed records. Those addresses will still be blocked until their individual timers expire naturally.

Rug Shield

A real-time exit system that monitors your open positions for danger signals and sells automatically if one is detected.

Rug Shield runs after you have already entered a position. It operates independently from your Stop Loss and Take Profit settings — think of it as a last-resort emergency exit triggered by on-chain events rather than price movements.

There are two independent Rug Shield modules. Both are on by default and can be toggled separately.

ModuleTriggerDefault
Honeypot ShieldDangerous on-chain instructions detected (freeze, authority change, new minting)ON
Dev Sell ShieldDeveloper wallet balance drops 15%+ from your entry pointON

Honeypot Shield

Watches on-chain program logs for instructions that indicate the token is being turned into a trap.

The bot opens a persistent WebSocket connection to Solana's logsSubscribe for the token's smart contract. It watches for these specific instructions:

InstructionWhat it means
FreezeAccountDeveloper freezes your wallet's ability to transfer the token
SetAuthorityOwnership or authority of the token program is being changed
MintTo / MintToCheckedNew tokens are being minted, diluting the supply

What happens when triggered: The bot immediately executes a 100% sell of your position. There is no delay or confirmation — speed is the priority.

Default: ON

When to turn it off If you are intentionally trading tokens with special tokenomics that involve minting (rebase tokens, certain algorithmic stablecoins), Honeypot Shield may trigger false positives. Disable it only for those specific cases.

Dev Sell Shield

Monitors the developer wallet's token balance and exits if they start dumping while you're holding.

How it works: The moment you enter a position, the bot records how many tokens the deployer holds. It then watches their balance continuously.

Trigger condition: If the deployer's token balance drops by 15% or more from where it was when you entered, the bot treats this as a developer dump in progress and sells your entire position immediately.

Example You buy a token. At that moment the dev holds 1,000,000 tokens. If the bot detects the dev's balance has fallen to 849,999 or lower (a 15%+ drop), it exits your position.

Default: ON

When to turn it off Some token setups involve the deployer wallet legitimately moving tokens to a vesting contract, a team wallet, or a DAO. If you know a particular project's deployer will be moving tokens for non-malicious reasons, you may want to disable this to avoid a premature exit.

When ON, the bot ignores your manually configured slippage and priority fee and calculates them dynamically at execution time.

How the Calculation Works

The bot runs two signals in parallel and uses the higher result of the two to ensure the transaction always lands.

Signal 1 — Market Signal (via DexScreener)

Looks at the token's current liquidity and recent price volatility.

Signal 2 — Network Signal (live chain data)

Queries the blockchain directly to see what fees are actually landing right now.

  • Solana: Calls getRecentPrioritizationFees and takes the 75th percentile fee from recent transactions. Converts this to a Jito tip floor in SOL.
  • EVM / Robinhood: Calls eth_feeHistory for the last 10 blocks and takes the 75th percentile priority fee. Final gas price = (2 × latest base fee) + p75 priority fee. The two-times buffer ensures the transaction doesn't expire if the base fee ticks up slightly.
The bot uses whichever signal produces the higher fee. If the market is calm but the network is congested (or vice versa), the bot still pays enough to land.

Exit Fee Escalation

Automatically increases fees on each retry when an auto-sell fails to confirm on the first attempt.

When a Stop Loss, Take Profit, Rug Shield, or Expiry sell fails to land, Exit Fee Escalation increases the fee on each subsequent retry. This ensures the sell eventually confirms even during periods of high network congestion.

Without this, a failed sell might keep retrying at the same low fee and never confirm — leaving you stuck in a losing position while the price continues to fall.

Default: ON

Recommendation Leave this ON. The only reason to disable it is if you need exact fee predictability on exits, which is rarely more important than actually getting out of a position.

EVM Gas Ceiling

A hard maximum gas price that the bot will ever pay on EVM / Robinhood trades, regardless of what Smart Fees calculates.

Range: 1.0 to 500.0 gwei
How to set: Tap the button and type a number. For example, 50 sets the ceiling at 50 gwei.

Why it matters

During extreme network congestion (such as a major market event), gas prices can spike dramatically. Without a ceiling, Smart Fees could calculate a very expensive gas price. The ceiling protects you from accidentally paying enormous fees on a single trade.

Trade-off

If the network is so congested that the required gas price exceeds your ceiling, the transaction will fail or be delayed. Set it high enough that you are rarely blocked, but low enough to protect against extreme spikes.

Fee Reserve

A minimum native token balance that must always remain in your wallet. The bot will not execute a trade if doing so would bring your balance below this amount.

ReserveTokenRange
◎ SOL Fee ReserveSOL0.000 – 1.000 SOL
Ξ EVM Fee ReserveETH / native EVM token0.000 – 1.000

Why it matters

Every trade costs gas fees paid in the native token (SOL or ETH). If your wallet is completely drained by trades, you would have no gas left to sell existing positions. The reserve ensures there is always enough native token to cover exit transactions.

Example If your SOL reserve is 0.01 SOL and your wallet has 0.012 SOL, the bot will not execute a buy — it would leave only 0.002 SOL, below the reserve. This protects your ability to sell existing positions.

Default: A small amount is pre-configured at setup. Adjust it based on how much you typically spend on fees per trade.

Adding a Channel

The Source Monitor is where you manage which Telegram channels or groups the bot watches for contract addresses.

How to add a channel

  1. Open the bot and tap Add Channel.
  2. Send the bot a link to any Telegram channel or group you want to monitor.
  3. The bot will begin watching that channel from that point forward.

Once added, the channel appears in the Source Monitor list. Tap a channel name to open and configure its settings.

Each channel has its own independent set of trading parameters — buy amount, slippage, stop loss, take profit, and filters. These per-channel settings override your global defaults only for trades triggered by that specific channel.

Guidance

  • Risk of high slippage: High slippage tolerance means you accept a worse fill price. It also makes you more vulnerable to sandwich bot attacks. Enable Anti-MEV to mitigate this.

Guidance

0.003–0.006 SOL covers most normal conditions. During heavy network load or for fast-moving tokens where being first matters, 0.01–0.025 SOL ensures your transaction lands ahead of others.

Anti-MEV

Routes your transaction through Jito's private bundle system to prevent MEV bots from seeing and front-running your trade.

Default: ON

What is a sandwich attack?

An MEV bot detects your pending buy in the public mempool, places its own buy just before yours (driving up the price), lets your buy fill at the inflated price, then immediately sells. Anti-MEV makes your transaction invisible to these bots until it confirms.

Recommendation Keep Anti-MEV ON, especially when trading tokens with limited liquidity where sandwich attacks are most profitable for attackers. There is no meaningful downside to leaving it enabled.

Stop Loss

Automatically sells your position when the price drops by a set amount after you enter.

Stop Loss has three modes: None, Once, and Segmented. All modes share the same Expiry timer and Auto-Sell toggle.

None

Stop Loss monitoring is disabled. Once you buy a token, the bot will not sell based on price drops. You would need to sell manually from the Positions menu.

Once

Sets a single price drop percentage. When the token's price falls that far below your entry price, the bot sells 100% of your position immediately.

OptionDetails
Presets5% / 10% / 15% / 20% / 25% / 30% / 50%
CustomTap "Custom %" and enter any number
Example You buy at $1.00 and set Stop Loss Once at 20%. If the price falls to $0.80 (a 20% drop from entry), the bot sells everything.

Segmented

Sells your position in multiple chunks at different price levels, rather than selling everything at once. You can create up to 6 segments.

How to set it up: After selecting Segmented, add segments one at a time. For each segment specify:

  • The % drop from entry that triggers this sell (e.g., -15)
  • What portion of your remaining position to sell at that level (e.g., 50%)
Example setup
  • Segment 1: Price drops 15% → sell 50% of position
  • Segment 2: Price drops 25% → sell 100% of remaining
If the price drops 15% you reduce your exposure. If it continues falling to 25% you exit completely. If it recovers after segment 1 fires, you still hold the other 50%.

Expiry

Sets a time limit on how long the bot monitors your position for Stop Loss (and Take Profit — they share the same timer). After the expiry time passes, monitoring stops.

OptionDetails
PresetsCommon hour values shown as quick-tap options
Custom HoursType any number between 1 and 240 (10 days maximum)
Default120 hours (5 days)

Expiry does not sell your position when it triggers — it just stops watching. To sell automatically at expiry, use the Auto-Sell toggle below.

Auto-Sell Toggle

When ON, the bot executes a full (100%) sell of your position the moment the Expiry timer runs out, regardless of whether any price target was hit.

Default: OFF

When to use this: If you are trading short-term setups and do not want to hold a position indefinitely, enabling Auto-Sell with a set expiry (e.g., 24 hours) ensures you always exit within your time window.

Take Profit

Automatically sells your position when the price rises by a set amount after you enter.

Take Profit has three modes: None, Once, and Trailing Stop. All modes share the same Expiry timer and Auto-Sell toggle as Stop Loss.

None

Take Profit monitoring is disabled. The bot will not sell based on price gains.

Once

Sets a single price increase percentage. When the token's price rises that far above your entry price, the bot sells 100% of your position.

OptionDetails
Presets10% / 25% / 50% / 100% / 200% / 500% / 1000%
CustomTap "Custom %" and enter any number
Example You buy at $1.00 and set Take Profit Once at 100%. If the price reaches $2.00, the bot sells everything.

Trailing Stop

Instead of selling at a fixed price, the bot continuously tracks the highest price the token reaches after your entry. If the price then retraces (falls back) from that peak by a set percentage, the bot sells.

This allows you to capture upside beyond a fixed target — if a token keeps climbing, your effective exit price climbs with it.

Retrace %

How far the price must fall from the peak to trigger the sell.

OptionDetails
Presets10% / 15% / 20% / 25% / 30% / 50%
CustomEnter any percentage
Example (20% retrace) Token peaks at $2.00. The sell triggers if the price falls to $1.60 or below (20% below the $2.00 peak).

Activation Price

A minimum gain from entry before the trailing stop begins tracking the peak. Until this gain is reached, the bot does nothing — it waits for the token to rise enough before arming the trailing logic.

OptionDetails
Presets+10% / +20% / +50% / +100% / +200%
None"Activate immediately" — trailing begins tracking from the moment of entry
Example (50% activation + 20% retrace) You buy at $1.00. The trailing stop does not arm until the price reaches $1.50. Once armed, the bot tracks the peak and sells if the price drops 20% from wherever the peak ends up.
Why use Activation? Without activation, a token that dips 20% right after you buy (before any gain) would trigger a trailing sell. Setting an activation level ensures you only trail after a meaningful gain — protecting you from normal early volatility.

Segmented Take Profit

Same concept as Segmented Stop Loss but for price increases. Set up to 6 levels, each selling a portion of your remaining position at a different gain percentage.

Example setup
  • Segment 1: Price rises 50% → sell 25% (lock in initial profit)
  • Segment 2: Price rises 100% → sell 50% of remaining (take more at 2×)
  • Segment 3: Price rises 300% → sell 100% of remaining (full exit at 4×)

Market Cap Min

The token's current market cap must be at or above this value at the moment the trade would execute.

Presets: $1K / $5K / $10K / $50K / $100K / $500K / $1M / $5M | Custom: enter any USD value

Use case: Filters out tokens so early-stage they have no meaningful price discovery yet.

Market Cap Max

The token's market cap must be at or below this value. Lets you focus on earlier-stage tokens and avoid buying into tokens that have already run significantly.

Presets: $100K / $500K / $1M / $5M / $10M / $50M / $100M

Liquidity Min

The token's trading pool must contain at least this much liquidity in USD. Low liquidity means high price impact and is a common characteristic of scam tokens.

Input: Type a USD value. Example: 5000 means the pool must hold at least $5,000.

Recommended minimum: $5,000–$10,000. Very low liquidity (under $1,000) means even small buys will move the price significantly.

Holders Min

The token must have at least this many unique wallet addresses holding it. Very few holders can indicate a fresh launch that hasn't spread to real buyers yet, or a concentrated position that's easy to dump.

Input: Type a number. Example: 100 requires at least 100 holders.

Top 10% Max

The top 10 holders combined cannot hold more than this percentage of the total supply. High concentration in few wallets means a coordinated dump could devastate the price.

Input: Type a percentage (0–100). Example: 60 means the top 10 wallets cannot collectively hold over 60% of supply.

Dev Holding Max

The developer/creator wallet cannot hold more than this percentage of the total token supply. A dev holding a large percentage of supply is a significant dump risk.

Input: Type a percentage (0–100). Example: 15 means the dev cannot hold more than 15% of supply.

LP Burn Min

At least this percentage of the liquidity pool (LP) tokens must be burned or permanently locked. When LP tokens are not burned, the developer can withdraw the liquidity at any time (a "rug pull"), crashing the token price to near zero.

Presets: 80% / 90% / 95% / 100% | Custom: enter any percentage

Recommended: 90–100% if rug protection is important to you. Many legitimate long-term projects burn 100% of LP.

Token Age Min

The token must have been deployed at least this many hours ago. Very new tokens (under 1 hour old) are often in the most dangerous phase — early snipers and dev bots are most active in the first few minutes.

Presets: 1h / 2h / 6h / 12h / 24h / 48h / 3d / 7d | Custom: enter any number of hours

Honeypot Toggle

Runs a honeypot detection check before buying. A honeypot is a token designed so you can buy but not sell — the sell function is deliberately broken or gated.

ON: Tokens flagged as honeypots are blocked. | OFF: No check.

Keep this ON at all times unless you have a very specific reason to trade a token that a honeypot scanner flags incorrectly.

Mintable Toggle

Checks whether the token's contract allows new tokens to be minted after the initial supply. A mintable token can have its supply inflated at any time, diluting the value of existing holders.

ON: Tokens where minting is still possible are blocked. | OFF: No mintability check.

Freeze Auth Toggle

Checks whether a freeze authority exists on the token. A freeze authority allows the token deployer to freeze your specific wallet's token balance, preventing you from selling.

ON: Tokens with an active freeze authority are blocked. | OFF: No freeze authority check.

Pause monitoring of a channel without removing it or losing any of its settings.

When a channel is disabled, the bot completely ignores all messages from it. Tap Disable Channel to toggle it off. Tap again to re-enable it. All your configured settings (buy amount, slippage, filters, etc.) are preserved exactly as you left them.

When to use this If you want to temporarily stop trading from a specific source — for example, during a period when you know the channel's calls are low quality — without having to set everything up again later. Use Disable instead of Remove whenever possible.
Disable vs. Remove Disabling a channel preserves all its settings and can be reversed. Removing a channel is permanent and deletes all settings. Always use Disable if you might want to re-enable the channel later.

Filter Mode

Controls whose messages in a channel the bot will process. This applies before any content analysis — messages from users who don't pass the filter are never scanned for contract addresses.

All Messages

The bot processes every single message posted in the channel, regardless of who sent it.

Use this when: The channel is a personal alert channel you control, or every member of the group is trusted to post calls.

Admin Only

The bot only processes messages sent by administrators of the channel or group. When a new message arrives, the bot checks whether the sender is in the admin list. Non-admin messages are silently ignored.

Use this when: You follow a channel where the main caller is an admin but other members also post, and you only want to act on the admin's signals.

Specific User

The bot only processes messages from a predefined list of specific Telegram User IDs that you provide.

Use this when: You have a group where only certain trusted individuals post actionable calls, or you want to whitelist yourself in a private group.

How to find a user's Telegram ID Message @userinfobot and forward a message from the person whose ID you want. The bot will reply with their numeric user ID.

Dynamic Filters

Content rules that a message must satisfy before the bot considers trading the contract address found in it.

If a message contains a valid contract address but fails your dynamic filters, the trade is aborted. This is useful when a channel posts many types of signals and you only want to trade when specific conditions in the message are met.

How filtering works

Every dynamic filter you add is checked against the message text. The logic is:

  • If any single filter passes → trade proceeds
  • If none pass → trade is cancelled
  • Individual filters use OR logic with each other
  • A Multi-Filter group uses AND logic internally (all conditions in the group must pass)

See the sub-pages for full instructions on adding single filters and multi-filter groups.

Add Filter

A single-condition dynamic filter that reads a real message from your channel and checks one number or word before trading.

This is a brand new type of filter — nothing else works like this. Instead of blocking keywords or requiring an exact static phrase, Add Filter reads a structured message, finds a specific value inside it, and checks whether that value meets your rule. If yes, the trade proceeds. If no, it's blocked.

When to use Add Filter vs Multi-Filter
Use Add Filter when you have one condition to check (e.g. token age under 3 minutes, or rating is "High"). Use Multi-Filter when you need multiple conditions to all be true at once. A single Add Filter is simpler to set up and easier to debug.

The three things you always provide

Every filter is built from three pieces. Understanding them is the key to getting this right.

PieceWhat it isHow to pick it
Context A line (or partial line) copied from a real post. Gives the bot the surrounding text to look at. Copy the smallest slice of the post that contains your value — but nothing else that could vary.
Anchor The fixed text sitting directly next to your value. Acts as a location guide — the bot finds the anchor, then reads what's right next to it. Must never change between posts. Must be immediately before or after the value. Spaces, emojis, and hyphens all count.
Value The actual number or word you want to check (e.g. 3 for token age, early for a signal label). This is what changes post to post. Everything else in your template should be fixed.

Choosing a good context

This is the most important step. Your context must contain the value you want to filter — but only that value should be able to change. Any other number or word that varies between posts will confuse the bot.

ContextResultWhy
MC $23.2K | ⌛ 3m ❌ Bad Two values change: the market cap (23.2K) and the age (3). The bot can't anchor cleanly to either.
| ⌛ 3m ✅ Good Only the age number changes. The | ⌛ part and the trailing m are always there — perfect anchor material.
| ⌛ 3 ❌ Bad Missing the unit m means the bot could match 3h (3 hours) in another format and you'd buy a token that's hours old.
The only thing that should change in your context is the value you're filtering. Anchor text, punctuation, emojis, units — all of it must stay fixed between every post the channel sends.

The command format

Tap Add Filter, then send the /dynamic command. Your context goes first, then you wrap the anchor in [ ] and the value in [ ]:

/dynamic <context> [anchor] [value]

The bot will read what comes after the anchor, extract the value, and apply your rule to it.

Example 1 — Numeric filter (token age)

Goal: Only buy tokens that are under 3 minutes old.

Your channel posts messages that include a line like:

| ⌛ 3m

The age number (3) changes. Everything else — the pipe, the hourglass, the space, the m — stays fixed.

Step 1 — pick your context. Use the shortest slice that contains only the age:

| ⌛ 3m

Step 2 — identify the anchor. The anchor is the hourglass emoji plus the space right before the number. Include the space:

⌛

Step 3 — the value is the number itself:

3

Your command:

/dynamic | ⌛ 3m [⌛ ][3]
Note on the space inside the brackets
[⌛ ] includes a trailing space. That space is part of the format in the post — the bot needs it to locate the number precisely. Copy exactly as it appears.

The bot will confirm "numeric filter mapped" and ask for your rule. For token age under 3 minutes, enter:

< 4

Why < 4 and not < 3? Because < 3 means "below 3" — it would miss tokens that are exactly 3 minutes old. < 4 catches 1m, 2m, and 3m.

Example 2 — Numeric filter (time since open)

Goal: Only buy tokens that have been trading for under 5 minutes.

Your channel posts a line like:

5 MIN after open

You might think after is the anchor — it's right next to the number. But look carefully: sometimes the format uses < 5 MIN (with a less-than sign before the number), so after isn't always directly beside the value. MIN, however, is always there and always in the same position.

Use MIN as the anchor instead:

/dynamic 5 MIN [MIN] [5]

Rule:

< 5.1

Using 5.1 instead of 5 ensures that a value of exactly 5 still passes (since 5 < 5.1 is true). With this rule the bot buys when it sees 1 MIN, 2 MIN, 3 MIN, 4 MIN, or 5 MIN.

Anchor placement matters more than it looks. If anything in the post format can shift the anchor away from the value — a symbol, a sign, an extra word — pick a different anchor that stays reliably adjacent.

Example 3 — Text filter (keyword)

Goal: Only buy when a post says the token was "promoted very early."

Your channel posts a line like:

the first community call initiated (promoted very early)

The word that changes is early (it could be late, fast, etc.). The word right before it — very — never changes in this position.

Anchor: very (include the trailing space)
Value: early

/dynamic the first community call initiated (promoted very early) [very ][early]

For the rule, type the word you want to allow:

early

Allowing multiple variations

If the post could say "promoted very early", "promoted very late", or "promoted very fast" and you want to buy all three, separate the allowed words with commas in your rule:

early, late, fast

If instead the variations change more than one word — for example "promoted very early", "really late", or "quite late" — then move your anchor further back to a word that stays fixed across all of them, like promoted:

/dynamic the first community call initiated (promoted very early) [promoted ][very early]

Rule:

very early, really late, quite late
Text rules are checked against the extracted value exactly. The rule early matches a post where the value is early. Separate multiple allowed values with commas. The check is case-insensitive.

Multi-Filter (AND Logic)

A group filter where every condition must be true in the same message before the bot trades. One failure blocks the whole group.

Add Filter checks one thing. Multi-Filter checks multiple things at once — and all of them must pass. If a post meets 2 out of 3 conditions, the trade is still blocked. This is how you make the bot precise: not just "this channel posted something," but "this channel posted exactly the kind of signal I'm looking for."

Same building blocks, multiple lines
Each condition in a Multi-Filter is built exactly the same way as an Add Filter — context, anchor in [ ], value in [ ]. The only difference is that you send all conditions together in one command, and the rules are set one per line.

The command format

Tap Add Multi-Filter (AND), then send /multidynamic followed by one filter line per condition. Each line is the same bracket format from Add Filter:

/multidynamic
<context line 1> [anchor1] [value1]
<context line 2> [anchor2] [value2]
<context line 3> [anchor3] [value3]

The bot will then ask for your rules — one rule per line, in the same order as your conditions.

Full example — 3 conditions, one trade

Goal: Only buy when a post shows all three of the following at once:

  • It is the first call on this token (calls count = 1)
  • Market cap is under $50K
  • The token was promoted very early

Your channel posts messages that contain lines like these:

CALLS - 1
Market Cap: $82K
the first community call initiated (promoted very early)

You need three filter lines — one for each condition. Build each one the same way you would for Add Filter:

#What to filterContextAnchorValue
1 First call only CALLS - 1 CALLS - 1
2 Market cap number Market Cap: $82 Market Cap: $ 82
3 Promotion label the first community call initiated (promoted very early) very early

Your full command:

/multidynamic
CALLS - 1 [CALLS - ][1]
Market Cap: $82 [Market Cap: $][82]
the first community call initiated (promoted very early) [very ][early]
Why does the market cap context stop at the number, not include "K"?
Sometimes market caps are in the millions — the format changes. Stopping before the unit (K, M) keeps the context reliable no matter how large the value is. The anchor Market Cap: $ is always there; the unit is not.

Setting the rules

After sending the command the bot confirms each filter and asks for your rules. Enter one rule per line in the same order as your conditions:

< 2
< 51
early
LineRuleMeaning
1 < 2 Calls count must be below 2 — meaning only 1 call exists (the first).
2 < 51 Market cap number must be below 51. Since the number in the post is in thousands (e.g. 82 = $82K), this catches anything under $51K.
3 early The text extracted after the anchor must match "early".

A post must satisfy all three to trigger a trade. Two out of three is not enough.

Combining single and multi-filters

You can run single filters and multi-filter groups at the same time. The overall logic is:

OR at the top level — AND inside each group.
If any single filter passes, the trade proceeds. If any multi-filter group has all its conditions met, the trade proceeds. A group where even one condition fails does not count.

This lets you define a simple fallback and a stricter full-signal condition at the same time. For example:

  • Single filter: SOL volume > 80 (basic momentum check)
  • Multi-filter group: first call AND under $50K market cap AND promoted early (full signal)

The bot trades if either condition set is satisfied — the basic check alone is enough, and so is the full signal alone. They don't need to both be true.

Watch for contradictions. If you have multiple active filters, make sure they don't conflict with each other. For example, a single filter requiring "Rating: High" and a multi-filter requiring "Rating: Low" would cancel each other out on any given post. It is fine to run multiple filters as long as you understand that any one passing is enough to trigger a trade.

Remove Channel

Permanently deletes the channel from the bot's monitoring list and erases all settings associated with it.

This action cannot be undone. All configured settings for the channel — buy amount, slippage, stop loss, take profit, filters — will be permanently deleted.

If you just want to pause a channel temporarily, use Disable Channel instead. Remove Channel should only be used when you are certain you will never want to trade this channel again.

Lists all active Solana token positions. Each position shows the token address and the current monitoring status.

Sell 100%

Tapping Sell 100% on a position immediately sends a market sell order for your entire holding of that token. The sell executes at the current market price regardless of where you are relative to your entry.

When to use this

  • You want to exit a position manually before the Stop Loss or Take Profit fires
  • You want to close a position that has no SL/TP configured
  • The monitoring expired and you are deciding whether to exit

Robinhood Positions

Lists all active EVM / Robinhood chain positions.

Sell 100%

Same function as the Solana equivalent — immediately sells 100% of the position at the current market price on the EVM chain.

Dust positions on EVM On EVM chains, if a position shows a very small value (dust), selling may cost more in gas than the position is worth. In that case you may choose to leave it rather than paying gas to close it. You can remove a position from tracking without selling by using the close/remove option for that position if available.
